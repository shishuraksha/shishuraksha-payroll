Hospital Attendance Rules Test Scenarios

Scenario 1: Normal Attendance

Employee with 3 offs (under limit)

Present Days (P): 20
P+OT Days: 2
Offs: 3
Absent: 1
OT Only: 4
Total Working Days: 20 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 2(P+OT extra) + 1(unused off) - 1(absent) = 31 days

Calculation Breakdown:

  • Base days: 20(P) + 2(P+OT) + 3(paid offs) = 25
  • Overtime days: 4(OT) + 2(P+OT overtime portion) = 6
  • Unused offs: 4 - 3 = 1 paid day
  • Total: 25 + 6 + 1 - 1(absent) = 31 days

Scenario 2: Exceeded Offs

Employee with 6 offs (exceeds 4 limit)

Present Days (P): 18
P+OT Days: 1
Offs: 6 (2 unpaid)
Absent: 2
OT Only: 3
Total Working Days: 18 + 1 + 4(paid offs) + 3 + 1(P+OT extra) + 0(no unused) - 2(absent) = 25 days

Warning:

Employee has taken 6 offs but only 4 are paid. The extra 2 offs are unpaid leave.

Scenario 3: P+OT Heavy Month

Employee with multiple P+OT days

Present Days (P): 15
P+OT Days: 8 (counts as 16 days pay)
Offs: 4
Absent: 0
OT Only: 3
Total Working Days: 15 + 8 + 4 + 3 + 8(P+OT extra) + 0(no unused) - 0 = 38 days

Note:

Each P+OT day provides 2 days of payment, making this a high-earning month due to overtime work.

Scenario 4: Perfect Attendance

Employee with no offs taken

Present Days (P): 26
P+OT Days: 0
Offs: 0 (4 unused)
Absent: 0
OT Only: 4
Total Working Days: 26 + 0 + 0 + 4 + 0 + 4(all unused offs) - 0 = 34 days

Bonus:

Employee receives payment for all 4 unused offs, effectively getting paid for 34 days in a 30-day month!

Hospital 24-Hour Attendance Rules Summary

Off Days Policy

  • • Maximum 4 paid offs per month
  • • Offs beyond 4 are unpaid
  • • Unused offs are paid extra
  • • System warns when exceeding 4 offs

P+OT (Present + Overtime)

  • • Counts as 2 days payment
  • • 1 day regular + 1 day overtime
  • • Common in 24-hour hospitals
  • • Higher earnings for staff

Payment Calculation

  • • Base: P + P+OT + Paid Offs
  • • Add: OT days + P+OT overtime
  • • Add: Unused paid offs (4 - taken)
  • • Subtract: Absent days
